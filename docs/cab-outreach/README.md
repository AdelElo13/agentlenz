# CAB Outreach — Quick Reference

**Goal:** Sign 10 Customer Advisory Board members with Letters of Interest (LOIs).
**Offer:** 70% lifetime discount in exchange for feedback, usage, and serving as a reference.
**Target:** Engineering leads / developers at companies spending $1K+/mo on AI API costs.

## Where to Find Prospects

### X/Twitter
Use the queries in `x-search-queries.md`. Focus on:
- People who have tweeted about AI API bills, token costs, or budget concerns in the last 30 days
- People actively building with LLM APIs (posting code, sharing agent demos)
- Founders/eng leads at AI-native startups (look at who replies to AI infra threads)

**Workflow:**
1. Run a search query
2. Filter to "Latest" tab (not Top)
3. Check the profile — do they have a technical role? Are they at a company that would have real AI spend?
4. Read their recent tweets before DMing — reference something specific in your DM

### LinkedIn
- Search: `("AI engineer" OR "ML engineer" OR "engineering lead") AND ("LLM" OR "AI agents" OR "OpenAI")`
- Filter: 51–500 employees, posted in last 30 days
- Look at who comments on posts from Andreessen Horowitz, a16z, Sequoia AI content
- Check who follows accounts like @swyx, @karpathy, @simonw — these are builders

### Reddit
- r/MachineLearning, r/LocalLLaMA, r/OpenAI, r/LangChain
- Search for "API costs", "billing", "expensive", "token budget"
- People posting "is this normal?" about AI API bills are perfect prospects
- DM with Reddit DM or reply to their post first to warm up

### Hacker News
- Search `site:news.ycombinator.com AI API cost` on Google
- Look at "Who's Hiring" threads for companies listing LLM/AI roles
- Find threads about LLM cost optimization — people who comment are self-selecting

## Notes on Tone

These people get a lot of outreach. What works:
- Be specific — reference something they actually said or built
- Be honest about where you are (early, building, need real feedback)
- Lead with their problem, not your solution
- Short messages > long messages on X
- Never use "I'd love to pick your brain" or "circle back" language
